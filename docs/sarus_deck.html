Why

What the model answers

As blending mandates, CORSIA, ETS and carbon prices tighten through 2050, three questions decide the SAF market — SARUS answers all three, year by year.

  • How much SAF each region needs — built bottom-up from real flight activity, not a top-down curve.
  • Where least-cost capacity gets built, in which pathway, and at what levelised cost.
  • What price clears each regional market — and how much demand falls back to CORSIA offsets.
Scope

One model, the full horizon

A single dynamic simulation spanning the global market, resolved annually with capacity that accumulates from one year into the next.

Design

Three design choices

Bottom-up

Demand from flights

Fuel burn is derived from route activity, fleet efficiency and route mix — so demand responds to policy, traffic growth and technology, not an assumed trajectory.

Spatial

Regional equilibrium

Least-cost supply meets each region's willingness-to-pay, with inter-regional trade and transport cost clearing the market.

Dynamic

Modular & typed

Four independent modules pass typed data contracts (Pydantic). Each year's capacity state feeds the next.

Step 1 · Demand

Bottom-up flight demand

Fuel = flights × distance × efficiency × (1 − r)year−2025 r = annual fleet-efficiency improvement (adjustable). SAF demand = fuel × applicable SAF share.
  • Two modes — a single global CORSIA schedule, or per-route SAF% targets interpolated across key years.
  • International split 60 / 40 origin / destination, per the CORSIA uplift rule.
  • Domestic routes optional; adjustable efficiency rate r tests technology scenarios.
Step 2 · Capacity

Least-cost capacity expansion

A linear program builds the cheapest new plants needed to close each year's supply gap, ranking candidates by levelised cost.

  • Objective — minimise total levelised cost of new build (LCOSAF-ranked).
  • Constraints — regional feedstock availability and refinery co-processing caps.
  • Costs — annual CAPEX, processing OPEX and feedstock cost per region & pathway.
Economics

LCOSAF — the cost spine

One metric ranks every pathway and sets the investment-floor price used throughout supply and pricing.

LCOSAF = ( CRF(IRR, life) × CAPEX + OPEXproc + OPEXfeed ) / Utilisation OPEXfeed = feedstock cost × feedstock intensity (t feed / t SAF). CRF annualises capital at the region's target return.
Step 3 · Willingness to pay

What each region will pay

WTP = max( Case 1, Case 2, Case 3 ) — the binding case reveals the dominant price driver.

Case 1 · Market floor

Jet + CORSIA

Jet-fuel price + carbon-credit value avoided: credit × 3.1 tCO₂ per MT of SAF.

Case 2 · Investment floor

LCOSAF @ IRR

Minimum price that makes new capacity viable — the cheapest pathway's levelised cost.

Case 3 · Market ceiling

Compliance + premium

Full price airlines pay: ETS / LCFS / mandate compliance value plus a regional premium.

Step 4 · Clearing

Cheapest supply meets highest WTP

PHASE 1
